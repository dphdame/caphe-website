# CAPHE Website - Product Context

## Purpose
CAPHE exists to bridge the gap between academic health economics methods and practical public health decision-making in California's 57 counties. The website serves as the primary platform for delivering educational content and building community among practitioners.

## Problems Solved

### For Policy Analysts & Epidemiologists
- Lack of accessible training in causal inference methods
- Difficulty evaluating program effectiveness rigorously
- Need for California-specific case studies and examples

### For the Organization
- Scalable content delivery beyond in-person workshops
- Member engagement and retention through exclusive content
- Lead generation through free community tier

## User Experience Goals

### Methods Lab
- **Progressive disclosure**: Public labs introduce concepts, community labs go deeper
- **Preview-with-gate pattern**: Non-members see partial content with clear CTA to join
- **Seamless authentication**: Login redirects back to intended content

### Membership Flow
1. Visitor discovers content through public labs
2. Encounters community-gated content, sees preview
3. Creates free account to unlock community content
4. Engages with community content, sees value
5. Applies for professional membership for full access

## Content Tiers

### Public Labs (12 labs)
Foundational concepts anyone can access:
- What Is a Counterfactual?
- The Before-After Trap
- Why Control Groups Aren't Enough
- Correlation ≠ Causation
- etc.

### Community Labs (13 labs)
Advanced methods requiring free account:
- Difference-in-Differences
- Interrupted Time Series
- Case Studies (CHW, Medicaid)
- Reading Regression Tables
- Cost-Effectiveness concepts
- etc.

### Professional Labs (3 labs)
Exclusive to professional members:
- Case Study: Comparing Programs
- Sensitivity Analysis
- CEA Under Uncertainty
