About Working Groups

Working groups are members-only collaborations where economists develop shared resources together. Current groups focus on cost-effectiveness methods, causal inference applications, and educational content.

The outputs of these working groups—white papers, code repositories, and analytical tools—are shared publicly. We believe in advancing the field through open resources.

Not a member? You can still benefit from our work by joining our listserv to receive notifications when new resources are published.

Who Can Join

Eligibility Requirements

CAPHE membership is specifically designed for trained economists working in public health. This focus ensures our peer review sessions, working groups, and discussions operate at the appropriate technical level.

Membership Criteria

To be eligible for CAPHE membership, you must meet at least one of the following:

  • 1. PhD in Economics or a related quantitative field (health economics, agricultural economics, public policy with economics focus)
  • 2. Master's degree with formal training in econometrics, causal inference, or economic evaluation methods
  • 3. Current enrollment in a PhD program in economics or a related field

Note on Scope

We focus on economists rather than epidemiologists or public health professionals who use economic methods in their work. This distinction helps us maintain the methodological depth of our peer review and working group discussions. If you're unsure whether you qualify, please apply and describe your economics training.

Not an Economist? Join as an Affiliate

CAPHE Affiliates are public health professionals, researchers, and others interested in health economics who don't meet the Member eligibility criteria. Affiliates stay connected to CAPHE's work without access to economist-only sessions.

Affiliate Benefits:

  • White paper and resource announcements
  • Tool release notifications
  • Event updates (1-2 emails/month)
