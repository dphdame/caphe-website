**Dimensions:** 800 x 500px

**Prompt:**
```
Clean, professional line chart on white background showing the "before-after trap" in program evaluation.

**Chart elements:**

X-axis: "Time" with labels "Before Program" (left half) and "After Program" (right half)
Y-axis: "Health Outcome (% improved)" ranging from 0 to 80

**Two trend lines:**

1. **Solid teal line (#0D9488, 3px):** Actual trajectory
   - Starts at 30% on far left
   - Rises steadily to 45% at program start (marked with vertical dashed line)
   - Continues rising at SAME SLOPE to 60% at end
   - Data points shown as filled circles

2. **Dashed amber line (#F9A825, 2px):** "What they claim"
   - Horizontal line at 45% before program
   - Jumps to 60% after program
   - Suggests a 15-point "program effect"

**Vertical dashed line (#757575):**
- At midpoint, labeled "Program Launches"
- Separates before/after periods

**Annotations:**

- Arrow pointing to the gap between lines at the end
- Text box: "Claimed Effect: +15 points" with small X mark (red #D32F2F)
- Below: "Actual Effect: 0 points — the trend was already happening" in italic gray (#757575)

- Small callout box near the pre-period trend: "Pre-existing upward trend" with arrow

**Style notes:**
- Grid lines very light (#F5F5F5)
- The comparison between the two interpretations should be visually striking
- Montserrat font throughout
- Flat design, no shadows or 3D effects
```

## Image 2B: Alternative Futures

**Filename:** `alternative-futures.png`

**Dimensions:** 800 x 450px

**Prompt:**
```
Clean, professional diagram showing multiple possible counterfactual trajectories on white background.

**Chart structure:**

X-axis: "Time" with "Before" and "After" sections
Y-axis: "Outcome"

**Lines:**

1. **Solid dark blue line (#0041A5, 3px):** Observed outcome
   - Rises from 40 to 60 over the period
   - Label: "What we observed"

2. **Multiple dashed lines branching from the program start point**, showing possible counterfactuals:

   a. **Dashed gray line (#9E9E9E):** Continues at same pre-trend slope
      - Label: "If trend continued"

   b. **Dashed teal line (#0D9488):** Levels off (plateau)
      - Label: "If improvement stopped"

   c. **Dashed amber line (#F9A825):** Declines
      - Label: "If regression to mean"

**Shaded uncertainty region:**
- Light gray shading between the highest and lowest counterfactual lines
- Label: "Range of 'what might have happened'" in italic

**Annotation:**
- Text at bottom: "Without a control group, we can't know which counterfactual is right" in 14px Montserrat, #757575

**Visual emphasis:**
- The branching point at program start should be highlighted (small circle)
- Fan-out of possibilities creates visual "uncertainty"
- Montserrat font, flat design, no shadows
```
