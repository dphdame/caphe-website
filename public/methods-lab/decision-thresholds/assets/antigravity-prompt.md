# Antigravity Prompt: Decision Thresholds Diagram

## Prompt

Clean, professional cost-effectiveness decision diagram on white background.
A conceptual illustration showing how a willingness-to-pay threshold divides programs into funded vs. not funded.

Main elements:
1. A coordinate system with "QALYs Gained" on x-axis and "Cost ($)" on y-axis
2. A diagonal threshold line from origin going up and right, labeled "$100K/QALY"
3. Two regions clearly distinguished:
   - Below the line: light green shaded area labeled "Cost-Effective (Fund)"
   - Above the line: light red/pink shaded area labeled "Not Cost-Effective (Do Not Fund)"
4. Three example program dots:
   - One green dot below the line (labeled "Program A")
   - One green dot below the line (labeled "Program B")
   - One red dot above the line (labeled "Program C")
5. A callout box explaining: "Programs below the threshold line have ICERs less than society's willingness to pay"

Visual style:
- Clean, flat design with no gradients or shadows
- Use rounded rectangles for labels (8px corners)
- Primary Blue (#0041A5) for threshold line and annotations
- Success Green (#2E7D32) for cost-effective region and dots
- Danger Red (#C62828) for not cost-effective region and dot
- Accent Gold (#F9A825) for the callout box border
- White background
- Montserrat font, semi-bold for labels

The diagram should clearly communicate the concept that the threshold line acts as a decision boundary, with programs plotted based on their cost and effectiveness, and their position relative to the line determining funding decisions.

- Cost-effective region: Success Green (#2E7D32) at 10% opacity fill
- Not cost-effective region: Danger Red (#C62828) at 10% opacity fill
- Cost-effective program dots: Success Green (#2E7D32) solid
- Not cost-effective program dots: Danger Red (#C62828) solid
- Callout box: Accent Gold (#F9A825) border, white fill
- Axis lines: Gray (#757575)
- Text: Dark (#1C1C1C)

## Key Visual Elements

1. **Threshold Line**: A clear diagonal line representing the $100K/QALY threshold, going from lower-left to upper-right
2. **Decision Regions**: The two regions should be immediately distinguishable - green for "fund" and red for "don't fund"
3. **Example Programs**: Show concrete examples of where programs might fall on the chart
4. **Clear Labels**: Every element should be labeled so the diagram is self-explanatory
