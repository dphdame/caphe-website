# Antigravity Prompt: Why "It Works" Isn't Enough DAG

## Prompt

Clean, professional directed acyclic graph (DAG) diagram on white background.
Four rounded rectangle nodes arranged in a diamond formation:

Top center: "Budget" in white text on blue background (`#0041A5`)
Left: "Program A" in white text on teal background (`#0D9488`)
Right: "Program B" in gray text (`#424242`) on light gray background (`#E0E0E0`)
Bottom center: "Health Outcomes" in dark text (`#1C1C1C`) on gold background (`#F9A825`)

Arrows:
- From "Budget" to "Program A": solid gray arrow (`#424242`) pointing down-left
- From "Budget" to "Program B": dashed gray arrow (`#757575`) pointing down-right, labeled "forgone"
- From "Program A" to "Health Outcomes": solid gray arrow (`#424242`) pointing down-right
- From "Program B" to "Health Outcomes": dashed gray arrow (`#757575`) with "?" label pointing down-left

Style: Flat design, no gradients, no shadows. Minimalist infographic style.
Montserrat font, semi-bold weight. Arrows have small arrowheads.
Nodes have 8px rounded corners and generous padding.

The key visual story: spending on Program A means not spending on Program B. The dashed lines show the counterfactual path that never happened. The question mark on Program B's effect emphasizes uncertainty about what outcomes would have been.
