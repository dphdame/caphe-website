About the Access Explorer

The CAPHE Medi-Cal Provider Access Explorer measures phantom network gaps across California's 58 counties by cross-referencing two federal datasets: the National Plan and Provider Enumeration System (NPPES) for directory-listed providers, and HHS Medicaid Provider Spending data for providers actively billing Medicaid.

Phantom networks are a documented barrier to healthcare access. Zhu et al. (2023, Health Affairs) found that 58.2% of primary care and mental health providers listed in Oregon Medicaid directories did not see Medicaid patients. The HHS Office of Inspector General (OEI-02-23-00540, 2025) found more than one-third of Medicaid-listed physicians did not provide care to enrollees in 2023.

CMS finalized network adequacy rules effective July 2025 (CMS-2439-F) requiring states to verify provider directories against actual utilization, making tools like this directly relevant to compliance monitoring.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a phantom network in Medi-Cal?

A phantom network refers to the gap between providers listed in Medi-Cal directories (registered in NPPES) and those actually seeing Medi-Cal patients (billing Medicaid). These phantom providers appear in directories but are not accessible to enrollees seeking care.

What data sources does the Access Explorer use?

The tool cross-references two federal datasets: the National Plan and Provider Enumeration System (NPPES) for registered providers, and HHS Medicaid Provider Spending data for providers actively billing Medicaid. This approach follows the methodology established by Zhu et al. (2023) and is consistent with CMS network adequacy verification requirements.

Which specialties are covered?

The tool covers six specialty categories: Primary Care, Behavioral Health, Dental, OB/GYN, Other Surgical, and Pharmacy & DME. Behavioral health typically shows the highest phantom rates statewide, consistent with the findings of Zhu et al. (2023).

How often is the data updated?

Data is updated quarterly as new HHS Medicaid spending data becomes available. The current dataset covers January 2018 through December 2024.
